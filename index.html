YouTube Trend Analysis

A guidance for YouTubers looking to launch and grow their channels.

Being a YouTuber seems like a fun and profitable profession. You create videos on topics that you like and make money off of them. However, becoming an established YouTuber is no easy feat. Behind every famous vlogger is a long journey of hard work. There are investments you need to make, processes you need to follow, and more before you

even create your first video. This dashboard provides basic analyses of global YouTube statistics for 2023. It aims to offer valuable guidance for beginners, helping you understand the key considerations when launching your own channel.

Content Category

Before embarking on your YouTube journey, it's crucial to determine the primary content category for your channel. Your content category defines what your channel will be all about and who your target audience is. Your choice can be guided by what you're passionate about, what you're knowledgeable in, or what you believe has an audience

demand. It's beneficial to explore trending content category on YouTube. These are areas where audiences are actively engaging, and there's potential for growth. In this section, we'll delve into a variety of trending content categories that can offer you valuable insights into where there's a demand for content.

Click on the bars to highlight a specific category.

The YouTube landscape is diverse, with a wide range of content categories. Among the top performing channels, "Entertainment" stands out as the most popular category, followed closely by "Music", "People & Blogs", "Gaming" and "Comedy". These categories tend to have substantial

following and a large number of viewers, performing better than other contents. It's also worth noting that other categories like "Education", "Film & Animation", "Howto & Style", "News & Politics", and "Science & Technology", also have a significant presence on the platform.

Except for a few of remarkable YouTubers who owns a large number of subscribers (highlighted in blue), most YouTubers in the popular categories fall within the subscriber range of 12,300,000 to 120,000,000. However, there are exceptions within "Comedy" and "Science & Technology" where channels have relatively fewer subscribers. Although there are many trending YouTube channels that produce "Comedy" content, individual channel performance in terms of subscriber counts is relatively modest. This suggests that while "Comedy" content is trending, it might not always translate into massive subscriber numbers for individual creators.

Likewise, when it comes to income, the earnings of YouTubers in specific content categories do not always align with the popularity of the content or the number of subscribers. Notably, categories such as "Gaming", "Howto & Style", and "Science & Technology" tend to, on average, generate less income compared to YouTubers creating content in other categories. This discrepancy may be due to the varying monetization opportunities available in each content category. For example, "Howto & Style" content with high audience engagement, such as product reviews or lifestyle content, may attract more sponsorships and affiliate marketing opportunities.

Location

Another vital factor to keep in mind when setting up your YouTube channel is your target audience niche. An integral component of defining your niche is identifying the particular

country or region for which your content will be customized. This geographical focus can significantly impact the content you create and the audience you attract.

The map provided above offers summary of the top 20 countries with the highest number of YouTube users. Understanding this data is crucial because the number of users directly influences the potential size of your audience.

Notably, India leads the way with the most YouTube users, boasting an impressive 467,000,000 users, followed closely by the United State.

The map above illustrates the distribution of trending YouTubers in each country. The United State leads with the highest number of trending YouTubers, followed closely by India. This pattern is in alignment with the number of YouTube users in each country, indicating the significance of

audience size. However, language diversity also plays a crucial role. One possible explanation for the United State having more popular YouTubers is that English content can be understood by a broader international audience compared to content in specific regional languages like Tamil .
